# Welcome
Hey folks, welcome to the first post of what I hope will be an active blog/crossword repository. To be quite honest, I'm not feeling amazing right now, especially with the ongoing COVID-19 outbreak/pandemic. I've just recently heard that my classes are online for the rest of the semester and my commencement/graduation ceremony is cancelled. While this is quite depressing, I do want to point out that at times like this, it's always great to look at the faint remnants of the silver lining that encompasses this whole situation. <br><br>

I'm currently surrounded by family (although we are constantly 6 feet apart!), and I have a number of friends to talk to. The situation right now calls everyone to employ "social distancing" - while this seems quite emblematic of what introverts/CSE students do, it doesn't necessarily have to be that way! Social distancing does not equal introversion nor does it equal shutting yourself in socially. Being in contact with your family and your friends (in a safe manner) is the way to go during these times. <br><br>

In fact, I get the notion that the whole outbreak provides the opportunity for everyone to tighten their relationships with their close ones as it serves as a reminder that nothing is truly guaranteed. It also gives one the opportunity to give time for themselves; I've definitely felt better after having some "me time", from watching movies, TikToks, and YouTube.

# Plan of action
With that being said, I'd like to enumerate some habits from the CDC that will help curb the spread of COVID-19 and ultimately "flatten the curve".

<blockquote cite="https://www.cdc.gov/coronavirus/2019-ncov/prepare/get-your-household-ready-for-COVID-19.html">
    <ul>
    <li>Avoid close contact with people who are sick.</li>
    <li>Stay home when you are sick, except to get medical care.</li>
    <li>Cover your coughs and sneezes with a tissue.</li>
    <li>Clean frequently touched surfaces and objects daily (e.g., tables, countertops, light switches, doorknobs, and cabinet handles) using a regular household detergent and water.</li>
    </ul>
    <footer><cite>Centers for Disease Control and Prevention</cite></footer>
</blockquote>

I wish the best of luck to everyone going through these tough times, and I have faith that we can all get through it together!
